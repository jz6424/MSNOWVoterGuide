Issues & Positions
01

Immigration

Amit Reyes — Democrat

Reyes distinguishes between legal and undocumented immigration, calling for humane treatment of all immigrants and a clear path to legal status for undocumented individuals. He supports sensible border policies that uphold human dignity above all.

So, I want to support for more humane treatment of legal immigrants ... I want to make sure they receive a humane treatment but still are deported.

Immigration and illegal immigration should be two separate matters. Humane treatment of illegal immigrants and giving them a better way to acquire legal status is imperative to proper immigration policy.

Meredith Kane — Republican

Kane takes a restrictive stance, calling for reduced asylum quotas, tighter border enforcement, and a strict permanent bar on re-entry for those who entered illegally. She frames undocumented immigration as an acute national crisis demanding urgent action.

Implement policy that if you are in America illegally you cannot come back and need to make income for America.

It's very much true. America is undergoing a crisis right now. I've seen over 13 illegal — 13 million illegal immigrants in our country right now. More are coming in every single day.

02

Women's Rights & Abortion

Amit Reyes — Democrat

Reyes supports federal protections for reproductive choice, arguing states should not have unchecked authority over abortion access. He favors increased federal funding for women's health clinics and national legislation ensuring consistent access for all women.

I believe federal implementation programs — more funding towards clinics and centers — and federal legislation and policies that make sure that states don't have too much freedom over what they can choose. I want women around the country to have the choice whether they have a child or not, and not leave it to the state's choice.

Meredith Kane — Republican

Kane supports a national abortion ban with limited exemptions built in. She frames broad federal restrictions as the appropriate policy model, acknowledging that some exceptional circumstances warrant carve-outs.

But I still do believe in general this like blanket policies [on banning abortion] that include those exemptions across the United States.

03

Welfare & Healthcare

Amit Reyes — Democrat

Reyes argues that affordable healthcare is a basic right. He highlights the real cost of inadequate coverage — workers staying on the job injured rather than seeking care — and frames the current system as both morally indefensible and economically unsustainable.

Every American should have the right to receive healthcare without having to fear losing their homes to pay for it. People would work while injured rather than getting healthcare because they can't afford it. It's not morally right and not sustainable.

Meredith Kane — Republican

Kane emphasizes individual self-reliance, opposing expanded welfare or government-provided healthcare. Her position centers personal responsibility as the primary mechanism for addressing economic hardship.

I believe that we should make everyone pull themselves up by their own bootstraps.

04

Gun Rights

Amit Reyes — Democrat

Reyes affirms the Second Amendment while calling for stricter requirements for concealed carry — including enhanced training and thorough background checks. He argues that collective public safety must be weighed against individual gun rights.

We should not prioritize the rights of one over the safety of many.

Meredith Kane — Republican

Kane advocates for expanding gun rights and increasing police firepower, opposing restrictions on magazine capacity and assault-style weapons. Her philosophy holds that escalating force — for civilians and law enforcement alike — is the most effective deterrent.

Force should always be met with more force. Give police bigger guns.

You know, the answer to a stick is a bigger stick, right? I believe in giving Americans more rights to use their firearms, reducing magazine capacity laws, reducing assault weapon laws, but giving the police a better capability to respond with heavy weaponry.

05

Labor & Economy

Amit Reyes — Democrat

Reyes supports raising the minimum wage as a direct response to inflation and as a tool to address labor shortages. He views higher wages as both a matter of workers' dignity and a practical economic stabilizer.

Raising the minimum wage [is needed] to help struggling families through the inflation in the past years. The issue of staff shortages can be remediated in corroboration with raised wages.

Meredith Kane — Republican

Kane opposes unionization, claiming it distorts labor markets and inflates wages in ways that harm worker mobility. She defends supply-side economic policy and rejects calls for wage increases, arguing the current model is already working.

Trickledown economics is wildly successful, we don't have to make any changes about wages.

06

Foreign Policy

Amit Reyes — Democrat

Reyes favors redirecting American foreign engagement toward humanitarian aid for populations affected by conflict. He supports maintaining U.S. global influence but wants to fundamentally reform how that influence is exercised.

Focus on sending more resources on humanitarian aid to help those affected by the war. Still maintaining influence but changing the way that influence is administered.

Meredith Kane — Republican

No public statement on record.

Candidate has not commented on this issue.
